This page provides a **neutral, verification-first** structure you can use when collecting and reviewing alleged leaks.

## 2) Verification checklist (under 10 minutes)

1. Capture the original claim URL and timestamp.
2. Save an archive snapshot (and note archive URL).
3. Identify whether the source is primary, secondary, or anonymous relay.
4. Run reverse image/video checks on visual evidence.
5. Look for at least two independent, credible corroborations.
6. Record one strongest confirming fact and one strongest disconfirming fact.
7. Mark confidence as Low/Medium/High and explain why in one sentence.

## 3) Red-flag patterns

- Claims that rely on broad group blame without verifiable evidence.
- Documents with no provenance, no metadata, or broken chronology.
- Narratives that mix factual details with speculative leaps.
- Content that asks for immediate sharing before verification.

## 4) Suggested output format for a neutral brief

- **What is known**: verifiable facts only.
- **What is unverified**: explicit uncertainty labels.
- **What is likely false or misleading**: with evidence.
- **What to monitor next**: concrete, falsifiable indicators.

## 5) One-line standard

> If a claim cannot be sourced, corroborated, and contextualized, treat it as unverified and do not present it as fact.
